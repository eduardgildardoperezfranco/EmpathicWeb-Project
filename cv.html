Perfil Ocupacional

Elite Executive Protection & Security Specialist Counter-Terrorism | Defensive & Evasive Driving | Explosive Risk Mitigation | Bilingual (English–Spanish) Elite Executive Protection and Security Specialist formed through a progressive, doctrine-based security trajectory that integrates military engineering, counter-terrorism, explosive risk management, humanitarian operations, and executive protection, developed across Colombian National Army institutions, Military Engineering Schools, Ministry of Defense programs, and internationally aligned private security academies. The professional foundation originates in formal military training within the Colombian National Army, where specialization in urban combat and counter-guerrilla operations established operational discipline, situational dominance, and tactical decision-making in asymmetric threat environments. This phase provided early exposure to non-linear conflict dynamics, irregular warfare, and population-centered security challenges— core competencies later transferred to executive and diplomatic protection contexts. Advanced formation as a Military Engineering Specialist in Explosives and Demolitions (E.X.D.E.) introduced a high-risk technical and ethical domain: the controlled handling, analysis, and neutralization of explosive threats. This training historically responds to Colombia’s long-standing internal conflict and positioned the professional to understand explosive devices not only as weapons, but as systems, including initiation methods, blast effects, structural impact, and prevention strategies. Subsequent certifications in commercial explosives for mining and civil works expanded this knowledge into civilian infrastructure protection and industrial risk environments. Training in Humanitarian Demining and Mine Action, reinforced through peace operations institutes and Ministry of Defense programs, marked a critical transition from purely kinetic roles to human-centered security and post-conflict operations. This formation emphasizes international humanitarian principles, civilian protection, and long-term risk reduction—competencies directly applicable to diplomatic missions, NGOs, and high-responsibility executive environments operating in fragile states. Specialized seminars in underwater blasting techniques and continuous updates in explosives management reflect exposure to complex engineering scenarios, reinforcing analytical thinking, procedural discipline, and compliance with strict safety and accountability standards. Complementary training through INDUMIL (Colombian Military Industry) in night defense systems, surveillance, and early-warning technologies strengthened expertise in integrated security systems, bridging physical protection with technological enablers. Parallel to military engineering formation, advanced preparation in anti-terrorism prevention against explosive devices and anti-personnel mines consolidated a preventive mindset: identifying indicators, disrupting attack planning cycles, and implementing layered protection models rather than reactive force. This doctrine underpins modern executive protection and diplomatic security frameworks. Transitioning into private and executive security, formal certification as Executive Protection Agent (Escolta)— including basic, advanced, and recurrent training—expanded operational scope to principal-centered protection, discretion, mobility planning, and close-protection ethics. Specialization in defensive and evasive driving, including armored and high-performance vehicles, reflects training developed in response to kidnapping, ambush, and urban attack patterns historically present in Latin America, now globally relevant in executive risk management. Additional specialization in protection of high-profile individuals (PMI), tactical motorbike operations, and security supervision reflects readiness to operate within complex protection teams, coordinate movements, and manage risk across dynamic environments without escalating threat visibility. Medical certifications in tactical medicine, basic life support, and pre-hospital emergency care (APH) add a critical survivability layer, enabling immediate medical intervention during attacks, accidents, or medical emergencies—an essential competency in modern close-protection doctrine. Formally recognized as a Victim of the Armed and Political Conflict in Colombia, a condition rooted in decades of national political disorder and internal violence. This status is not incidental but formative, reinforcing a deep ethical commitment to the protection of life, civilian security, and conflict-sensitive operations. It provides lived understanding of political violence, civilian vulnerability, and the long-term consequences of insecurity—an experiential asset in diplomatic, humanitarian, and high-responsibility protection roles. Complementing operational formation, advanced professional education through the systems thinking, and applied technology contributes a strategic, analytical, and architecture-oriented mindset. This education strengthens adaptability to technology-integrated security ecosystems, intelligence-informed planning, and future-oriented risk mitigation without limiting professional identity to a technical role. Bilingual professional with experience in multinational, corporate, diplomatic, and high-exposure environments, delivering ethically grounded, intelligence-driven, and discreet security solutions aligned with international best practices, human-security principles, and executive-level expectations. .
